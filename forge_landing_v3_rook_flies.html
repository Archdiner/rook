Method

Three things happen,
in this order.

i

She is sent.

You provide a URL and a read-only PostHog key. She arrives quietly and begins reading thirty days of every click, every scroll, every moment of frustration captured in your data.

~ five minutes
ii

She reads.

Where visitors rage-click. Where they stop scrolling. Which button they think is interactive but isn't. Where every non-converter died, and what was the last thing they touched. She is unbothered by aesthetics — she cares only about why people leave.

~ forty hours
iii

She returns with notes.

Three findings, ranked by leverage. Each tied to evidence in your own data, with the exact element identified and a suggested change. Not "consider testing a question headline." This specific sentence, on this specific button, by Tuesday.

~ two days

Most software is sold by being faster. We are not interested in being faster. The internet does not need a faster way to pick a button colour.

What it needs, we think, is something with the patience to actually look. To sit with a website for thirty days. To notice the pattern that the keeper, busy with the rest of their life, has not had time to see.
